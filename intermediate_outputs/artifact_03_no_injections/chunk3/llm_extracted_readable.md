# LLM Deviation Review

## REG-028
- Clause: Art18(1)
- Considered References: Art21(1)

- Deviation Count: 1

- Deviation 1
  - Type: Execution style deviation
  - GDPR Quote: The data subject shall have the right to obtain from the controller restriction of processing where one of the following applies:
  - Policy Quote: Right to restrict processing: You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal data.
  - Mismatch: GDPR grants a right to obtain restriction when conditions apply, while the policy frames it as a right only to request restriction.
  - Analysis: The wording downgrades a guaranteed entitlement ('obtain') to a mere request, potentially implying discretionary acceptance by the controller. Conditions (a)-(d) are omitted but omissions are treated as Non-Deviation.

## REG-025
- Clause: Art17(1)
- Considered References: Art6(1), Art9(2), Art21(1), Art21(2), Art8(1)

- Deviation Count: 1

- Deviation 1
  - Type: Data deviation
  - GDPR Quote: The data subject shall have the right to obtain from the controller the erasure of personal data concerning him or her without undue delay ... where one of the following grounds applies: (a) the personal data are no longer necessary ...; (b) the data subject withdraws consent ... and where there is no other legal ground for the processing; (c) the data subject objects to the processing pursuant to Article 21(1) and there are no overriding legitimate grounds ... or the data subject objects to the processing pursuant to Article 21(2); (d) the personal data have been unlawfully processed; (e) the personal data have to be erased for compliance with a legal obligation ...; (f) the personal data have been collected in relation to the offer of information society services referred to in Article 8(1).
  - Policy Quote: Right to erasure: You have the right to request that we erase your personal data if and to the extent that the data is no longer needed for the purposes for which it was collected or, if the processing is based on your consent, you have withdrawn your consent.
  - Mismatch: The policy limits erasure to two grounds (no longer needed; consent withdrawn) and omits other GDPR grounds such as objection under Art 21(1)/(2), unlawful processing, legal obligation to erase, and ISS to a child.
  - Analysis: The phrase 'if and to the extent that ... or ...' reads as limiting rather than illustrative. This narrows the scope of erasure grounds compared to Art 17(1) and its cross-references (Art 6(1), Art 9(2), Art 21(1)/(2), Art 8(1)).

## REG-027
- Clause: Art17(3)
- Considered References: Art9(2), Art9(3), Art89(1)

- Deviation Count: 1

- Deviation 1
  - Type: Severity deviation
  - GDPR Quote: Paragraphs 1 and 2 shall not apply to the extent that processing is necessary: (a) for exercising the right of freedom of expression and information; (b) for compliance with a legal obligation ... or for the performance of a task carried out in the public interest or in the exercise of official authority vested in the controller; (c) for reasons of public interest in the area of public health in accordance with points (h) and (i) of Article 9(2) as well as Article 9(3); (d) for archiving purposes in the public interest, scientific or historical research purposes or statistical purposes in accordance with Article 89(1) ...; or (e) for the establishment, exercise or defence of legal claims.
  - Policy Quote: A right to erasure does not exist insofar as • the data may not be deleted due to a legal obligation or must be processed due to a legal obligation; • the data processing is necessary for the assertion, exercise or defence of legal claims.
  - Mismatch: The policy lists only legal obligation and legal claims as exceptions, omitting freedom of expression, public interest in public health, and archiving/research/statistics exceptions allowed by GDPR.
  - Analysis: By stating only two exceptions, the policy is stricter (over-compliant) than GDPR, effectively expanding the erasure right beyond what GDPR requires. References to Art 9(2)(h),(i), Art 9(3), and Art 89(1) substantiate the missing exceptions.

## REG-021
- Clause: Art15(1)
- Considered References: Art22(1)

- Deviation Count: 1

- Deviation 1
  - Type: Negation deviation
  - GDPR Quote: The data subject shall have the right to obtain from the controller confirmation as to whether or not personal data concerning him or her are being processed, and, where that is the case, access to the personal data and the following information:
  - Policy Quote: The right to information does not exist or is subject to limitations if and to the extent that confidential information, such as information that is subject to professional secrecy, would be disclosed.
  - Mismatch: GDPR Art 15(1) grants a right of access/information, while the policy declares that this right does not exist or is limited when confidential/professionally secret information would be disclosed.
  - Analysis: Within the scope of Art 15(1) and its cross-reference to Art 22(1), no such limitation is stated. The policy introduces an additional condition that negates or limits the right beyond what is expressed in Art 15(1).
