# LLM Deviation Review

## REG-025
- Clause: Art17(1)
- Considered References: Art6(1), Art9(2), Art21(1), Art21(2), Art8(1)

- Deviation Count: 2

- Deviation 1
  - Type: Severity deviation
  - GDPR Quote: the data subject withdraws consent on which the processing is based according to point (a) of Article 6(1), or point (a) of Article 9(2), and where there is no other legal ground for the processing;
  - Policy Quote: if the processing is based on your consent, you have withdrawn your consent.
  - Mismatch: The policy grants erasure upon any withdrawal of consent without the GDPR’s qualifier that there must be no other legal ground for the processing.
  - Analysis: By omitting the condition “and where there is no other legal ground,” the policy is more generous than GDPR, expanding the circumstances under which erasure is promised.
- Deviation 2
  - Type: Data deviation
  - GDPR Quote: the controller shall have the obligation to erase personal data without undue delay
  - Policy Quote: we must stop processing your personal data and remove it from our IT systems and databases.
  - Mismatch: GDPR requires erasure of personal data generally, while the policy confines deletion to 'IT systems and databases,' narrowing the medium/scope.
  - Analysis: Specifying only IT systems and databases potentially excludes other media or locations of personal data, narrowing the scope relative to the GDPR’s medium-neutral erasure obligation.

## REG-021
- Clause: Art15(1)
- Considered References: Art22(1)

- Deviation Count: 1

- Deviation 1
  - Type: Data deviation
  - GDPR Quote: confirmation as to whether or not personal data concerning him or her are being processed
  - Policy Quote: whether and which personal data we store about you.
  - Mismatch: The policy narrows the Art 15(1) right from all processing to only what is stored.
  - Analysis: Limiting the confirmation to stored data excludes processing operations beyond storage, narrowing the GDPR’s broader 'are being processed' scope.
