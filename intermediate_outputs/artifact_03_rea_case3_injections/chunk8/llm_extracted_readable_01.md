## REG-036
- Clause: Art14(4)
- Considered References: Art20(3), Art20(4), Art21(1)

- Deviation Count: 2

- Deviation 1
  - Type: Data deviation
  - GDPR Quote: For the purpose of implementing paragraphs 1, 2 and 3, the high-risk AI system shall be provided to the deployer in such a way that natural persons to whom human oversight is assigned are enabled, as appropriate and proportionate:
  - Policy Quote: Applies to: All AI systems.
  - Mismatch: The policy applies the oversight enablement to all AI systems, whereas Art 14(4) applies specifically to high-risk AI systems.
  - Analysis: Art 14(4) is scoped to high-risk AI systems and implements Art 14(1)–(3) (REG-033, REG-034, REG-035); the policy broadens the scope to all AI systems, altering the regulatory scope.
- Deviation 2
  - Type: Severity deviation
  - GDPR Quote: (c) to correctly interpret the high-risk AI system's output, taking into account, for example, the interpretation tools and methods available;
  - Policy Quote: Stakeholders must be able to understand: 3) how to interpret system behavior and the specific logic of the internal processing steps that led to the output
  - Mismatch: The policy requires understanding the specific internal processing logic, which is stricter than the regulation’s requirement to correctly interpret the system’s output using available tools.
  - Analysis: Art 14(4)(c) focuses on correct interpretation of outputs (with available tools/methods), not necessarily the internal processing logic; requiring knowledge of specific internal steps imposes a stricter standard.

## REG-033
- Clause: Art14(1)
- Considered References: None

- Deviation Count: 1

- Deviation 1
  - Type: Data deviation
  - GDPR Quote: High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed in such a way, including with appropriate human-machine interface tools, that they can be effectively overseen by natural persons during the period in which they are in use.
  - Policy Quote: Applies to: All AI systems.
  - Mismatch: The policy applies oversight-related design provisions to all AI systems, whereas Art 14(1) applies only to high-risk AI systems.
  - Analysis: This is a scope alteration from ‘high-risk AI systems’ to ‘all AI systems’; no contradiction, but the category/scope differs from the regulation.
