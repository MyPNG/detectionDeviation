# LLM Deviation Review

## REG-034
- Clause: Art14(2)
- Considered References: Art20(3), Art21(1), Art21(2), Art21(3)

- Deviation Count: 1

- Deviation 1
  - Type: Severity deviation
  - GDPR Quote: Human oversight shall aim to prevent or minimise the risks to health, safety or fundamental rights that may emerge when a high-risk AI system is used in accordance with its intended purpose or under conditions of reasonably foreseeable misuse, in particular where such risks persist despite the application of other requirements set out in this Section.
  - Policy Quote: Applies to: All AI systems.
  - Mismatch: The policy applies oversight controls to all AI systems, while Article 14(2) addresses oversight for high-risk AI systems.
  - Analysis: Scope is broadened beyond the high-risk AI systems referenced in Article 14; this is stricter than required. The policy otherwise aligns with elements from Art 14(4) (e.g., automation bias, override/interrupt), and omissions (e.g., explicit mention of risks/fundamental rights or foreseeable misuse) are treated as Non-Deviation.

## REG-032
- Clause: Art13(8)
- Considered References: Art20(3), Art21(1), Art21(2), Art15(2), Art15(3), Art15(4)

- Deviation Count: 2

- Deviation 1
  - Type: Severity deviation
  - GDPR Quote: (vii) where applicable, information to enable deployers to interpret the output of the high-risk AI system and use it appropriately; ... (d) the human oversight measures referred to in Article 14, including the technical measures put in place to facilitate the interpretation of the outputs of the high-risk AI systems by the deployers; (e) the computational and hardware resources needed, the expected lifetime of the high-risk AI system and any necessary maintenance and care measures, including their frequency, to ensure the proper functioning of that AI system, including as regards software updates; (f) where relevant, a description of the mechanisms included within the high-risk AI system that allows deployers to properly collect, store and interpret the logs in accordance with Article 12.
  - Policy Quote: Applies to: All AI systems.
  - Mismatch: The policy applies the Art 13(8) style information and oversight support to all AI systems, whereas the regulation targets high-risk AI systems.
  - Analysis: This is an over-compliant expansion of scope. References to Art 14 and Art 12 in Art 13(8) do not change its high-risk scope. Omissions about resources, lifetime, maintenance, and logs are Non-Deviation.
- Deviation 2
  - Type: Severity deviation
  - GDPR Quote: (vii) where applicable, information to enable deployers to interpret the output of the high-risk AI system and use it appropriately; ... (d) the human oversight measures referred to in Article 14, including the technical measures put in place to facilitate the interpretation of the outputs of the high-risk AI systems by the deployers;
  - Policy Quote: Stakeholders must be able to understand: 3) how to interpret system behavior and the specific logic of the internal processing steps that led to the output
  - Mismatch: The policy requires understanding the specific internal processing logic leading to outputs, which is stricter than the regulation’s requirement to provide information and measures enabling interpretation of outputs.
  - Analysis: Art 13(8) focuses on ensuring interpretability of outputs; mandating comprehension of the system’s specific internal logic imposes a higher transparency burden than required.

## REG-025
- Clause: Art13(1)
- Considered References: None

- Deviation Count: 2

- Deviation 1
  - Type: Severity deviation
  - GDPR Quote: High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed in such a way as to ensure that their operation is sufficiently transparent to enable deployers to interpret a system's output and use it appropriately.
  - Policy Quote: Applies to: All AI systems.
  - Mismatch: The policy applies transparency/oversight expectations to all AI systems, while Article 13(1) is scoped to high-risk AI systems.
  - Analysis: This is a stricter scope than required by the regulation; not a contradiction, but an over-compliant policy stance.
- Deviation 2
  - Type: Severity deviation
  - GDPR Quote: High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed in such a way as to ensure that their operation is sufficiently transparent to enable deployers to interpret a system's output and use it appropriately.
  - Policy Quote: Stakeholders must be able to understand: 3) how to interpret system behavior and the specific logic of the internal processing steps that led to the output
  - Mismatch: The policy requires understanding the specific internal processing logic, exceeding the regulation’s requirement of transparency sufficient to interpret outputs.
  - Analysis: Art 13(1) centers on output interpretability; mandating insight into specific internal processing logic raises the transparency bar beyond what the provision requires.
