## REG-035
- Clause: Art14(3)
- Considered References: Art20(3), Art20(4), Art21(2), Art21(3)

- Deviation Count: 1

- Deviation 1
  - Type: Execution style deviation
  - GDPR Quote: The oversight measures shall be commensurate with the risks, level of autonomy and context of use of the high-risk AI system, and shall be ensured through either one or both of the following types of measures: (a) measures identified and built, when technically feasible, into the high-risk AI system by the provider before it is placed on the market or put into service; (b) measures identified by the provider before placing the high-risk AI system on the market or putting it into service and that are appropriate to be implemented by the deployer.
  - Policy Quote: When possible, design the system elements identified in A5.2. When this is not possible (for example, when Microsoft is not responsible for the system UX), provide guidance on human oversight considerations to the third party responsible for implementing the system elements identified in A5.2.
  - Mismatch: The regulation uses technical feasibility as the criterion for built-in oversight measures, whereas the policy substitutes organizational possibility (e.g., responsibility for UX) as the determinant.
  - Analysis: Art14(3)(a) hinges on 'when technically feasible' for provider-built measures; the policy’s 'when possible' example focuses on organizational responsibility (not being responsible for UX) to switch to guidance, changing the regulatory decision basis from technical feasibility to organizational constraints.
